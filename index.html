How do we use visual media to

Hi there! I'm a 5th-year PhD Candidate in Experimental Psychology at the University of California San Diego.

My research uses large-scale crowdsourcing techniques to benchmark how people create and interact with visual media. In my graduate research, I've specialized in conducting a wide range of visuospatial & generative behavioral tasks to study human visual communication in domains like sketching, abstract diagrams, and data visualizations. I've also conducted developmental research using drawing to study children's conceptual development of visual object concepts.

By developing quantative characterizations of these behaviors, I hope to help improve generative AI models mimicking human generative behaviors and human-centered tools aimed at supporting human-ai interactions related to: text-to-image-generation, audience design, object identification, and semantic segmentation

Bio: I grew up on Whidbey Island in WA State. As an introverted teenager on a rural island, I spent most of my time sketching and hiding away in ceramics studios. After 2 art apprenticeships and double majoring in Philosophy and History of Mathematics & Sciences from St. John's College, I became fascinated by how novel visual media (e.g., Cartesian coordinate system, periodic table, Vitruvian man) across history helped catalyze and communicate major scientific discoveries. Despite this critical role of visual media in human innovation, relatively little is known about the cognitive processes that underlie how people communicate via images, by comparison to language. This question inspired and continues to guide my research throughout my graduate program.

research

Below are three lines of work exploring visual communication across different domains:

What drives recognizability of different visual object concepts?

From children's drawings of common objects to adults' abstractions of categories and subcategories, I study how the semantic structure of our visualizations change across childhood and flexibly depending on our visual context. By analyzing large-scale datasets of 2K children's drawings to 12K adult drawings, my work aims to comprehensively explore how people choose to represent different visual object concepts. Our datasets will be publically released shortly!

How do explanatory visualizations convey higher-level knowledge?

Visual explanations are at the core of human innovation, collaboration, and communication. I study how people encode their knowledge about physical mechanisms into sketches. I then crowdsource semantic annotations through custom web browser experiments and use computer vision techniques to analyze the drawn content and its spatial organization to determine how people prioritize different kinds of visual information depending on their viewers' goals.

How do data visualization conventions guide interpretations?

Data visualizations are poweful tools to compress large data, reveal latent trends, and communicate vital knowledge to a wide audience. However, data visualizations are far from objective. For example, how might a scientist vs. a journalist flexible generate such visualizations? I study how people produce graphs in order to convey different aspects of statistical patterns.
